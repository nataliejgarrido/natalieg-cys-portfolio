# 🎣 PHISHING ANALYSIS: Rooms 1 – 7
**Completed:** April 2026<br>
**Difficulty:** Easy – Medium

## ROOM 1: Phishing Analysis Fundamentals

### Tools and Concepts Used
- Mail protocols (POP3, SMTP, IMAP)
- Email header and body analysis
- Types of phishing (spear, whaling, vishing, smishing, spam)
- Business Email Compromise (BEC)

## ROOM 2: Phishing Emails in Action

### Tools and Concepts Used
- Email and header body analysis
- Phishing techniques 
- URL shortening
- Branded HTML
- Spoofed email address, brand impersonation
- Attention-grabbing subject line (artificial urgency
- Pixel tracking
- Link manipulation, link redirection
- Credential harvesting
- Poor grammar and typos
- Blind carbon copy (BCC)

### Key Takeaways and My Thoughts
It was really interesting to see "real" phishing emails. Most of the emails shown were quite obvious in their malicious intent, but there are a lot of techniques used to maximize the phisher's goal. I was aware of most of these techniques, but BCC was new to me.

## ROOM 3: Phishing Analysis Tools

### Tools and Concepts Used
- Email header and body analysis
- Malware sandboxes
- ANYRUN sandbox analysis of malicious attachments
- VirusTotal
- PhishTool

### Key Takeaways and My Thoughts
I'm appreciative of the amount of tools available for malware and email analysis, especially the tools available for email header analysis. They just make everything easier!
